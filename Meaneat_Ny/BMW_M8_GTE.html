About the BMW M8 GTE

The BMW M8 GTE is a high-performance race car developed for endurance racing. It made its debut in the 2018 IMSA WeatherTech SportsCar Championship and has since become a symbol of BMW's motorsport heritage. Built for precision, speed, and durability, the M8 GTE combines cutting-edge aerodynamics, lightweight construction, and a powerful engine to deliver exceptional performance on the racetrack. With a turbocharged V8 engine, sophisticated suspension systems, and an advanced chassis, the BMW M8 GTE excels in long-distance endurance racing, including the prestigious 24 Hours of Le Mans and 24 Hours of Daytona. The car is engineered to compete in both the IMSA and WEC (World Endurance Championship) series. The BMW M8 GTE has become a fan favorite for its aggressive design, lightning-fast acceleration, and its ability to dominate on some of the world's most challenging race tracks. Engine Specifications:

  • Engine: 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8
  • Output: 500+ hp
  • Torque: 650+ Nm
  • Transmission: 6-speed sequential gearbox
  • Weight: Approx. 1,250 kg
  • Aerodynamics: Active aerodynamics for optimized downforce
  • Chassis: Carbon fiber monocoque for enhanced rigidity and lightweight performance
